# The aesthetic of the project

The aesthetic of the project is inspired by the evolution of Berlin's club culture. In its early years, clubgoers wore comfortable and functional clothing, often choosing oversized Adidas and Puma tracksuits that allowed complete freedom of movement while dancing. As clubbing became increasingly underground and, in some contexts, illegal, black gradually became the dominant color. Wearing black offered anonymity, protection, and a strong sense of belonging within the community.

Over time, two major aesthetic influences emerged within Berlin's club scene. The first was shaped by the LGBTQ+ community, introducing elements inspired by fetish and kinky culture, where the body became a medium for identity, freedom, and self-expression. The second drew inspiration from science fiction and cyberpunk aesthetics, embracing futuristic materials, wearable technologies, and a post-human vision of the body.

The design of my project seeks to bring together these historical references into a single visual language. The wearable device is not only functional but also reflects the visual identity of Berlin's club culture, combining sportswear influences with kinky and sci-fi aesthetics. In doing so, the project pays tribute to the history, values, and cultural identity of one of the world's most influential nightlife scenes.
